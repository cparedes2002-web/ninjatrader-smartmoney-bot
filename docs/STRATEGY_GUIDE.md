# Smart Money Concept Strategy Guide

## Overview

The Smart Money Concept (SMC) is a price action trading methodology that focuses on identifying and following the trades of institutional investors and large hedge funds.

## Core Concepts

### 1. Order Blocks
- **Definition:** Strong directional candles that represent institutional entry/exit zones
- **Bullish Order Block:** A strong bullish candle where institutions bought
- **Bearish Order Block:** A strong bearish candle where institutions sold
- **Usage:** Price retesting these blocks indicates potential entry points

### 2. Fair Value Gaps (FVG)
- **Definition:** Imbalances in price where the market "skipped" over a level
- **Cause:** Gap occurs due to sudden buying/selling pressure
- **Trading:** Market tends to "fill" or retest the gap
- **Example:** ES gaps up, then retraces to fill the gap

### 3. Liquidity Pools
- **Definition:** Price levels where many stop losses cluster
- **Location:** Previous highs and lows attract stop loss orders
- **Smart Money:** Moves price to trigger stops, then reverses

### 4. Market Structure
- **Higher Highs (HH):** Uptrend - each swing makes a higher high
- **Higher Lows (HL):** Uptrend - each swing makes a higher low
- **Lower Lows (LL):** Downtrend - each swing makes a lower low
- **Lower Highs (LH):** Downtrend - each swing makes a lower high

### 5. Structure Breaks
- **Bullish Break:** Price breaks above previous HH or HL
- **Bearish Break:** Price breaks below previous LL or LH
- **Signal:** Indicates institutional repositioning

## Entry Strategy

### Setup Requirements
1. **Structure Break** - Price breaks market structure
2. **Order Block** - Recent strong directional candle exists
3. **Volume Confirmation** - Above average volume on break
4. **FVG** - Fair Value Gap present for confluence
5. **Timeframe Confirmation** - Higher timeframe aligns

### Entry Rules

```
BULLISH ENTRY:
- Price breaks above previous high (HH or resistance)
- Volume > 1.5x average
- Close above break level
- Stop loss: Below recent low (order block)
- Target: 2:1 risk/reward minimum

BEARISH ENTRY:
- Price breaks below previous low (LL or support)
- Volume > 1.5x average
- Close below break level
- Stop loss: Above recent high (order block)
- Target: 2:1 risk/reward minimum
```

## Exit Strategy

### Profit Taking (Tiered)
```
50% Position: Take profit at 1:1 RR
30% Position: Take profit at 1.5:1 RR
20% Position: Take profit at 2:1 RR+ (runner)
```

### Stop Loss Management
```
Initial SL: Below order block low (bullish) / Above order block high (bearish)
Trailing SL: Move stop to breakeven after 1:1 RR
Time SL: Exit if no progress after 4 hours
```

## Risk Management Rules

1. **Max Risk Per Trade:** 2% of account
2. **Max Daily Loss:** 5% of account
3. **Max Position Size:** 1-2 contracts (50K account)
4. **Risk/Reward Minimum:** 1:1.5
5. **Position Sizing:** Fixed fractional method

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Trading without order block confirmation** → False breakouts
2. **Ignoring volume** → Weak moves trap traders
3. **Bad risk/reward ratios** → Losses exceed wins
4. **Over-leveraging** → Account blown out
5. **Trading during news** → Unexpected volatility
6. **Not using stops** → Catastrophic loss
7. **Trading outside liquid hours** → Wide spreads

## Timeframe Selection

**Recommended:** 5-minute bars for entries
**Confirmation:** 1-hour for trend direction
**Avoids:** 1-2 minute (too noisy)
**Extended Hold:** Can hold into 15-30 minute

## Trading Hours

**Best Hours (US Eastern):**
- 9:30 AM - 11:30 AM (Open, high liquidity)
- 1:00 PM - 3:00 PM (Afternoon, good volume)
- 3:30 PM - 4:00 PM (Close, re-entries)

**Avoid:**
- Pre-market (low volume)
- 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM (lunch, thin volume)
- After 4:00 PM (reduced volume)
- During news releases
